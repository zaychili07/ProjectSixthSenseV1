# BIRE Responsible Use and Data Policy

## Purpose
Project Sixth Sense – Bio-Intelligence Risk Engine (BIRE) is a research and educational project focused on early clinical deterioration detection and epidemiological risk modeling.

## Data Use
- This project should use only synthetic, anonymized, de-identified, or publicly approved research datasets.
- No personally identifiable information (PII) should be uploaded, stored, or processed in this repository.
- Any future real-world clinical deployment would require formal institutional approval, legal review, and regulatory compliance.

## Intended Use
- BIRE is intended as a decision-support and research system only.
- It is not a medical device, diagnostic authority, or replacement for licensed clinical judgment.
- Outputs from this system should be interpreted as probabilistic risk signals, not final medical conclusions.

## Safety and Ethics
- Model development should prioritize patient safety, transparency, and responsible evaluation.
- Special attention should be given to false negatives, bias, and unequal model performance across populations.
- Human oversight is required for any practical use of model outputs.

## Deployment Restrictions
- This repository is not approved for bedside, hospital, or public-health operational deployment.
- Any production use would require clinical validation, governance review, and security assessment.

## Contribution Expectations
By contributing to this project, contributors agree to:
- avoid uploading sensitive or identifiable patient data
- document assumptions clearly
- support transparent and ethical model development
- use the repository in a responsible and research-focused manner
